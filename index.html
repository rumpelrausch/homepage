About Rumpelrausch Täips

It all started in the early 90s.

The first CD burners were on the market, and my little home studio featured a heavy rack holding half a dozen second-hand midi expanders. At first it was all just a 4-track cassette recorder with one track holding a midi timecode, controlling an Atari ST with an early Cubase version. The archive format was a VHS cassette, later a DAT tape.

Most of the early tapes and the burned CDs were lost in time until I got my DAT walkman running again, so I resampled what I could salvage.

As a programmer I got interested in C++ and the creation of audio plugins. In 2000 the first instrument was released, "ZR-1", already containing most of the technologies later used for "Crazy Diamonds" and "AZR3". It was real fun to convert knowledge about analogue circuits into code.

Use any of this as you like. Consider a donation to some globally acting NGO / charity such as Medicines Sans Frontieres, Greenpeace or Amnesty International.

Have fun.
